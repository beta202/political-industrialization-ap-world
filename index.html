I. The 1848 Revolutions

A series of nationalist uprisings across Europe, including France, Germany, and Italy. Led to short-term voting reforms and influenced later democratic changes, such as universal male suffrage in France (1848).

II. The British Reform Acts (1832, 1867, 1884)

Gradually expanded voting rights in Britain, moving from landowning elites to middle-class men (1832), working-class men (1867), and rural men (1884).

III. The Women's Suffrage Movement (Late 19th – 20th Century)

Led to global recognition of women's suffrage, spreading across Europe, Latin America, and Asia throughout the 20th century.
